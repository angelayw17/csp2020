1.1.9. Project - Wacky Shapes and Colors

We created a program that made objects move in a pattern in an infinite loop.
First, we imported the Turtle and Math classes. After that, we created two empty lists for the sets
of shape objects that we were going to use, those being “left_shapes” and “right_shapes”. Then
we made lists for the shapes and colors of the objects that we used to initialize our objects. We
then added the shapes into their respective lists, colorized them, and moved them into their starting
positions, with the objects in “left_shapes”being facing the same direction and the objects in
“right_shapes” facing another direction. The shapes then moved forward and we established a
condition for what happens after the first collision. We then set up the collision. After the
shapes collided for the first time, they changed color and shape and moved in the opposite
direction. We stored the original colors and shapes of the objects in variables so that we could
revert them to their original conditions after the second collision. Because the objects followed a
triangular path, they switched directions again and began to move toward each other after
moving a predetermined distance after the first collision. After the objects collided for a second
time, they went back to their original position and restart original pattern for the next set of objects.



1.2.5. Project - Alien Rescue

This was the original outline:
Game has a UFO, aliens, and rocks. There is also a timer and a score component. The UFO can move
around using keyboard controls. The score increases when you “rescue” (collide with) an alien.
Depending on which game mode (survival or infinite), colliding with a rock will either end the game or
cause your score to decrease. In infinite mode, the timer is set to 9,999 seconds and in survival mode the
timer is about 1 minute.

That didn't really work, so here's the greatly simplified outline:
Game has one UFO turtle, one alien turtle, and a list of shapes for the alien turtle. There is still
a timer and a score component, the UFO still moves around using keyboard controls, and the score
still increases when you "rescue" (collide with) an alien. HOWEVER, there is only one game mode and zero
rocks (placement of rocks such that there was no overlapping between all turtles was too complicated).
To play the game, move the UFO around and try to rescue aliens. When you "rescue" an alien, your score
increases by 1 and another alien with a different shape appears in a different location. Keep rescuing
aliens until the timer runs out.



1.3.1. Project - Cannonball

Video of Project

We created the game Cannonball, in which a user controls and adjusts a cannon to shoot a
cannonball at targets moving across the screen. The targets are spawned at random heights at the right
boundary of the game screen and move at random speeds toward the left side of the screen. When the user
successfully hits a target, the target disappears, a new target is created, and the user's score increases.
Regardless of whether or not the cannonball hits or misses a target, it continues on its straight path
until it reaches the game's top or right boundary and is returned to it's original position at the cannon.
If a target reaches the left boundary, the game is over.
We also created four game modes: easy, normal, hard, and super hard. As the game modes increase in difficulty,
the targets get smaller in size and move more quickly, and the cannonball gets smaller in size.
